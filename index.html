What Does Your Zone Mean?

Moderate to Low Risk

Zones B and X

These areas are between the "100-year flood" and "500-year flood" zones. Flooding doesn't happen often, but it is still possible. Some of these areas are protected by levees which are big walls that hold back water.

Zone C

These are the safest zones! They're outside the main flood areas and are well protected. Small puddles or drainage issues might form after heavy rain, but serious flooding is very unlikely here.

High Risk

Zone A

There is a 1% chance of flooding every single year which might sound small, but over 30 years that adds up to a 1 in 4 chance. If you have a mortgage in this zone, the government requires you to have flood insurance.

Zone AE

Same as Zone A, but scientists have done more detailed studies here. They know exactly how high the water could get during a flood. This is one of the most common high-risk zones.

Zones AH and AO

These zones flood in shallow water, usually only 1-3 feet deep. AH zones flood like a pond, and AO zones flood in moving sheets of water, like a slow river spreading across flat land.

Zone AR

This zone is temporarily high-risk because a levee or dam nearby is being built or repaired. Once the construction is done, the flood risk should go back down.

Zone A99

A flood control system is being built here that will protect this area. Until it's finished, it will be considered high risk.

Zones V and VE

These are coastal high-risk zones near oceans or large lakes. They face flooding by very strong waves. These are some of the most dangerous flood zones.

Unknown Risk

Zone D

Nobody has studied this area closely enough to know its flood risk yet. Flood insurance is available but not required here.
