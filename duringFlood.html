What Should You Do During a Flood?

Listen to Alerts
Keep your phone or a battery-powered radio nearby. Follow instructions from local authorities because they have real time information.
Evacuate If Told To
If the officials say to leave, then go immediately. Take your emergency kit and only the most important items like money and identification.
Do not Walk in Floodwater
Even ust 6 inches of moving water can cause you to fall and get trapped under water. Floodwater can also be hiding sharp debris or open manholes underneath.
Don't Drive Through Water
Water can make your car float away, and if your car stalls, get out immediately and move to higher ground.
Stay Away from Power Lines
Stay far away from fallen power lines. If water enters your home, turn off the electricity only if it's safe to reach the breaker. Else, exit immediately.
If You're Trapped Inside
Move to the highest floor and signal for help from a window. Do not go into the attic unless you have a way out because the water might trap you in.

In an emergency call 911 · FEMA helpline: 1-800-621-3362
