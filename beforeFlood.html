What Should You Do Before a Flood?

Know Your Zone
Check your FEMA flood zone on the home page. If you are in a high-risk zone (A / V), take extra steps to prepare early.
Build an Emergency Kit
Pack a bag with 3 days of supplies: water (1 gallon per person per day), snacks, a flashlight, batteries, a first-aid kit, money, and any medications you need.
Make an Evacuation Plan
Plan two ways out of your neighborhood and a place to meet your family if you get separated, and practice it so everyone knows what to do.
Protect Important Documents
Put things like your ID, insurance papers, and family photos in a waterproof bag or box. Alternatively, you can also take photos of them and save them online.
Prepare Your Home
Move valuables off the floor to higher shelves. Know where your home's water shutoff valve is, and clear gutters and drains so water can flow freely.
Sign Up for Alerts
Sign up for your local emergency alerts so you get a text or call when a flood watch or warning is issued in your area.

In an emergency call 911 · FEMA helpline: 1-800-621-3362
