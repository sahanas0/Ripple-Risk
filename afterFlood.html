What Should You Do After a Flood?

Wait for the All-Clear
Only return home when officials say it is safe. The roads and bridges might look safe but they could be very damaged and dangerous underneath.
Floodwater is Dangerous
Floodwater is mixed with sewage, chemicals, and bacteria. Wear rubber boots and gloves if you have to walk through it. Wash everything it touched and dispose of it if it is too contaminated beyond repair.
Check Your Home Carefully
Before going inside, look for cracks in the walls or foundation. If you smell gas, stay out and call 911 from outside. Do not turn on electricity until a professional says it's safe.
Take Photos of Everything
Before cleaning up, photograph all the damage for your insurance claim. Make a list of everything that was damaged or destroyed.
Beware of Mold
Mold can start growing in 24 hours after a flood. Remove wet furniture and dry everything out as fast as possible using fans. Throw away anything soft that got soaked and cannot be cleaned.
Ask for Help
If your area was declared a disaster zone, you can apply for help at DisasterAssistance.gov or call 1-800-621-3362.

In an emergency call 911 · FEMA helpline: 1-800-621-3362
